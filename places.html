  • New York City

    New York City comprises 5 boroughs sitting where the Hudson River meets the Atlantic Ocean. At its core is Manhattan, a densely populated borough that’s among the world’s major commercial, financial and cultural centers.   • Paris

    Paris, France's capital, is a major European city and a global center for art, fashion, and culture. Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine. Beyond such landmarks as the Eiffel Tower, the city is known for its cafe culture, and landmarks like the Notre-Dame Cathedral.

  • Sydney

    Sydney, capital of New South Wales and one of Australia’s largest cities, is best known for its Sydney Opera House, with a distinctive sail-like design. At the harbour’s edge, the Sydney Harbour Bridge stretches 1,149 meters. The city is also famous for its beautiful beaches, such as Bondi Beach and Manly Beach.

  • Tokyo

    Tokyo, Japan's busy capital, mixes the ultramodern and the traditional, from neon-lit skyscrapers to historic temples. The opulent Meiji Shinto Shrine is known for its towering gate and surrounding woods. The Imperial Palace sits amid gardens and moats. The city is also famous for its bustling districts like Shibuya and Shinjuku.
